# Article scope

## Main claim
We present a model-based open-loop workflow for designing plate-temperature reference trajectories for cryostage freezing-front shaping, with explicit physical/operational admissibility constraints and a conservative practical carry-forward configuration.

## What the paper will show
- the full BO-based workflow
- the explicit cascade from trajectory parameters to freezing response
- the evidence-backed admissibility logic
- that schedule choice materially affects outcomes
- a conservative practical working default for downstream use

## What the paper will NOT claim
- that the globally optimal final parameterization has been proven
- that all remaining uncertainty about trajectory parameterization is closed
- that unsupported warming behavior is validated
